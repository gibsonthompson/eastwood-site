How to Tell If Your Water Heater Is on Its Way Out

Most tank water heaters last 8 to 12 years. Some of the signs that yours is getting close to the end:

  • Rusty or discolored water coming from the hot side
  • Rumbling, popping, or banging sounds from the tank
  • Visible corrosion or rust on the exterior
  • Water pooling around the base
  • Hot water doesn't last as long as it used to
  • Your unit is more than 10 years old

If you're seeing any of these, give us a call before it turns into a flood. A planned replacement is always cheaper and less stressful than an emergency one — but we handle both.

Water Heater FAQ

How much does it cost to replace a water heater in Gardner, MA?

A standard 40-50 gallon tank replacement typically runs $1,200 to $2,500 installed, depending on the type and any code upgrades needed. Tankless units cost more upfront but save on energy over time. We quote before we start — never after.

How long does it take to install a new water heater?

A like-for-like tank swap usually takes 2 to 4 hours. Converting from tank to tankless involves additional gas line and venting work, so that's typically a full-day job.

Tank or tankless — which is better?

It depends on your household. Tankless units never run out of hot water and last longer, but they cost more upfront and need a larger gas line. Tank units are simpler and cheaper to install. We'll help you figure out which one makes sense.
