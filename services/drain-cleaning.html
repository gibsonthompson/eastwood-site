We Clear Drains the Right Way

Chemical drain cleaners are a temporary fix at best. At worst, they eat through your pipes — especially the older cast iron and galvanized piping common in Gardner-area homes built before the 1970s. We use professional mechanical equipment to physically clear the blockage without damaging your plumbing.

We handle clogs in:

  • Kitchen sinks and garbage disposals
  • Bathroom sinks, showers, and tubs
  • Toilets and floor drains
  • Main sewer lines
  • Laundry and utility drains
  • Basement and foundation drains

Recurring Clogs Mean a Bigger Problem

If you're clearing the same drain every few weeks, the clog isn't the real issue — it's a symptom. Grease buildup in kitchen lines, hair accumulation in bathroom drains, and tree root intrusion into main sewer lines are the usual culprits. We diagnose the root cause so you're not paying to clear the same line over and over.

For main line issues, we can scope the line with a camera to see exactly what's happening underground before recommending any work. No guessing, no unnecessary digging.

Drain Cleaning FAQ

How much does drain cleaning cost?

Simple clogs run $150 to $300. Main sewer line work costs more depending on severity and access. We always give you a price before we start.

Should I use Drano or Liquid-Plumr?

We'd recommend against it. Those products can corrode pipes — especially older plumbing — and rarely solve the actual problem. A professional snake or hydro jet is safer and actually works.

Why does my kitchen sink keep clogging?

Usually grease. Even small amounts of cooking oil and fat coat the inside of your drain pipe over time, narrowing the opening until nothing gets through. A proper clearing removes the buildup, not just the clog.
