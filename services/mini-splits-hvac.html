Ductless Mini Split Systems

Many homes in Gardner, Westminster, Ashburnham, and the surrounding area were built long before central air conditioning was standard. Adding ductwork to these homes is expensive and disruptive. Ductless mini split systems solve that problem — they provide both heating and cooling through a compact indoor unit connected to an outdoor compressor, with no ductwork required.

Mini splits are ideal for:

  • Older homes without existing ductwork
  • Additions, finished basements, and bonus rooms
  • Room-by-room temperature control (zoning)
  • Replacing window A/C units with a permanent solution
  • Garages, workshops, and sunrooms
  • Homes looking to reduce heating and cooling costs

Mini Split & HVAC FAQ

How much does a mini split system cost to install?

A single-zone mini split typically costs between $3,000 and $5,000 installed. Multi-zone systems for whole-home coverage cost more but often qualify for Mass Save rebates that bring the price down significantly.

Can a mini split heat my home in winter?

Yes. Modern cold-climate mini splits are designed to operate efficiently down to -15°F or lower. They're a legitimate primary heat source for Massachusetts winters, not just air conditioners.

Do mini splits qualify for Mass Save rebates?

Many do. Massachusetts offers incentives for energy-efficient HVAC equipment through the Mass Save program. The exact rebate depends on the equipment model and your utility provider. We can help you figure out what you qualify for.

How long does a mini split installation take?

A single-zone installation typically takes 4 to 6 hours. Multi-zone systems may take a full day. We handle everything including electrical connections, line sets, and mounting.
