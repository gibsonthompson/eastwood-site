Why Convert from Oil to Gas?

Thousands of homes across North Central Massachusetts still run on oil heat. While oil systems work, natural gas is cleaner, more affordable, and more convenient. You'll never need to schedule a fuel delivery again, and gas prices are typically more stable than oil throughout the heating season.

Benefits of converting to gas:

  • Lower monthly heating bills — gas is consistently cheaper per BTU than oil
  • No more fuel tank maintenance, deliveries, or emergency fill-ups
  • Cleaner burning with fewer emissions
  • Modern gas boilers and furnaces run at 95%+ efficiency
  • Increased home value — buyers prefer gas heat
  • Gas line also powers stoves, dryers, fireplaces, and water heaters

What the Conversion Process Looks Like

An oil to gas conversion involves more than swapping equipment. We coordinate the full process so you don't have to manage multiple contractors:

  • Site assessment and gas availability check
  • Gas line installation coordination with the utility
  • Removal of your old oil tank (above or below ground)
  • New gas boiler or furnace installation
  • Chimney liner installation if required
  • All local permits and inspections
  • System testing and commissioning

Most conversions take 2 to 5 days depending on the scope. We schedule the work to minimize time without heat and keep you informed at every step.

Oil to Gas FAQ

How much does an oil to gas conversion cost?

A typical conversion ranges from $7,000 to $15,000 depending on whether you need a new gas line run to your property, the type of heating equipment you choose, and any chimney work required. We provide detailed estimates before starting.

Is natural gas available in my area?

Natural gas availability varies by street in the Gardner area. We check with the utility as part of our initial assessment at no charge. If gas isn't available at your property, we can discuss propane as an alternative.

What happens to my old oil tank?

We coordinate removal and proper disposal of your old oil tank. Underground tanks require soil testing and may involve additional remediation if there are any leaks. We handle the entire process.

How long will I be without heat during the conversion?

We schedule conversions to minimize downtime. In most cases, your heat is off for one day during the equipment swap. We avoid scheduling conversions during the coldest months when possible.
