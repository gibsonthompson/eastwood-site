Why Homeowners in Gardner Are Ditching Oil

The math speaks for itself. Natural gas costs less per BTU than heating oil, the price is more predictable, and you never have to worry about running out mid-storm because your delivery got delayed. Gas burns cleaner, requires less maintenance, and modern gas boilers run at 95%+ efficiency compared to 80-85% for most oil systems.

Beyond the savings, switching to gas opens up other options — gas stoves, gas dryers, gas fireplaces, and gas water heaters all become possible once the line is in. One conversion, multiple upgrades.

Oil to Gas FAQ

How much does an oil to gas conversion cost?

Typical range is $7,000 to $15,000 depending on gas line availability, the equipment you choose, and whether chimney work is needed. We give you a detailed breakdown before you commit to anything.

Is natural gas available on my street?

It varies block by block in the Gardner area. We check with the gas utility as part of our free assessment. If gas isn't available, we can discuss propane as an alternative.

What happens to my old oil tank?

We remove it and handle proper disposal. Underground tanks require soil testing — if there's a leak, we coordinate the remediation. Above-ground tanks are simpler and can usually be removed the same day as the conversion.

How long will I be without heat?

Usually about one day during the equipment swap. We schedule conversions to avoid the coldest months whenever possible and work to minimize the gap.
